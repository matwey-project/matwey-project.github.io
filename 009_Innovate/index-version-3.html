Children in need.

Making every intervention count.

Proven to de-escalate up to 80% of cases coming in for referral and
assessment, Innovate CYPS’ 14-week Children in Need (CIN)
programme significantly reduces the long-term reliance on CIN
plans, eliminates drift and identifies those children at risk, earlier.

Each time children’s services respond quickly with an appropriate
targeted intervention, it lessens the chances of a case being
escalated to child protection. Managed by a team of
highly-experienced senior social workers, the programme has one
main aim; to make sure each child receives the right intervention at
exactly the right time.

Sharing accountability.

The CIN programme involves Innovate CYPS taking full statutory case responsibility for all cases coming in for referral and assessment. Our social work practitioners, overseen by senior social work managers, are experienced at delivering tailored and intensive home-based intervention. Interventions that makes our CIN programme successful at de-escalating cases and securing better outcomes for children and young people.

Of course, we realise no two local authorities are the same, even though their statutory responsibilities are. That’s why, before assuming full statutory care responsibility, we take a deep-dive diagnostic review of your existing service to discover what your pain points are. This is so that we can co-produce a new way of working to make sure the changes we introduce at a service-level are sustainable to avoid future drift and delay.

Outcomes-focused.

Being outcomes-focused, we set very clear and measurable KPIs with you, which are routinely monitored, giving you the confidence that the programme is delivering measurable intervention to achieve sustainable change.

With the programme engaging multi-agency partners from the beginning we have a proven track record of successfully de-escalating up to 80% of families within the project timeframe.

Who we’ve helped.

We couldn’t be where we are today, making the difference we do for vulnerable children, young
people and their families - without the commitment from our clients to do things differently.

Our methodology

Whilst we do provide extra resource for local authorities, out CIN programme is not just about increasing capacity. It is about giving you the headspace and support to reframe entire referral and assessment services through introducing extremely targeted intervention. This is where our CIN Programme is different to any other solution. For a thorough overview of our CIN programme methodology, please download our service guide.
