The Challenge.

The London Borough of Enfield [LBE] children’s social care team needed a trusted partner to help them find a sustainable and cost-effective way of reducing the number of children on the edge of care from becoming Looked-After Children.

Like many local authorities, the children and young people identified by LBE as being on the edge of care are extremely vulnerable. They face the very real risk of child criminal exploitation, child sexual exploitation, knife crime, county lines and gang infiltration.

Our Approach.

Two 12-month programmes (overlapping by six months) have seen Innovate CYPS provide intensive support to two cohorts of 40 children and their families on the edge of care.

The programme offers an environment where social workers can meet their statutory obligations, whilst engaging with other professionals, services and community groups to empower families to make better life choices.

Bringing together experienced senior statutory social work and non-statutory multidisciplinary professionals, Innovate CYPS provides highly tailored one-to-one support to break the cycle.

The Results.

The results of the programme at LBE have been outstanding. Extensive engagement with the families and effective partner agency work is enabling positive outcomes. Outcomes that would not have been possible outside of the Edge of Care programme.

At the time of writing the case study, the programme was still ongoing. The results from the first cohort of 40 children, which completed the programme in July 2019, are extremely encouraging. There’s every indication Innovate CYPS is on track to meet the performance-related goal set by LBE. At the end of cohort 1, all cases had been progressed and concluded, with 20 closed to LBE.
